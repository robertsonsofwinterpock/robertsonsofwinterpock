LINKS

Official Clan Donnachaidh Web Site – This is the main website for the Robertsons and related Scottish clans worldwide.

Society of the Cincinnati – The descendents of officers of George Washington’s Continental Army (e.g., of Lt. John Robertson) are entitled to membership. William Robertson Lipscomb was a member. (See also http://www.hereditary.us/cin_history.htm and http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_the_Cincinnati.)

Chesterfield Historical Society – Both Winterpock and Moses M Robertson’s still standing farmhouse – where Benjamin Moses Robertson and Judith Anne Robertson Foster were born and lived during the Civil War - are located in Chesterfield County. The Chesterfield Historical Society is a treasure trove of information on the county of our ancestors.

Bermuda Hundred Seminar – On June 28, 2001, a seminar was conducted at the various sites of the Bermuda Hundred Campaign of 1864 in Chesterfield County not far from Moses M Robertson’s house. The events described in Judith Anne Robertson Foster’s letter to her grandchildren took place during and just after this Campaign. Anna Cox Aiken Robertson – age 8 - fled with her family from Varina Plantation during this campaign, never to return. This site contains photos from that seminar showing the battlefields and other sites. There is also an entire book on the Bermuda Hundred Campaign entitled Back Door to Richmond by William Glenn Robertson (Louisiana State University Press, 1987).
